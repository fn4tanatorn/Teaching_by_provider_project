Pharmacogenomics — High-Yield Summary
=====================================

1. Core Concept
---------------
Pharmacogenomics = study of how genetic variation affects drug response.

It explains variation in:
- Drug efficacy
- Drug toxicity
- Drug metabolism
- Drug transport
- Drug dosing requirements

Main goal:
- Use genetic information to improve drug selection and dosing.
- Reduce adverse drug reactions.
- Support precision medicine.

2. Important Terms
------------------
Allele:
- One of several alternative forms of a gene.

SNP:
- Single nucleotide polymorphism.
- A one-base DNA sequence change.

Coding SNP:
- SNP in a protein-coding region.

Nonsynonymous SNP:
- Coding SNP that changes an amino acid.

Synonymous SNP:
- Coding SNP that does not change an amino acid.

Haplotype:
- A group of linked alleles inherited together.

Copy number variation:
- Variation in the number of copies of a gene segment.

Phenotype categories:
- PM = poor metabolizer
- IM = intermediate metabolizer
- EM = extensive/normal metabolizer
- UM = ultrarapid metabolizer

3. Pharmacogenomics and Precision Medicine
------------------------------------------
Precision medicine uses patient-specific information to guide therapy.

Examples:
- Avoiding codeine in CYP2D6 ultrarapid metabolizers
- Avoiding clopidogrel in CYP2C19 poor metabolizers
- Avoiding abacavir in HLA-B*57:01-positive patients
- Adjusting warfarin dose using CYP2C9 and VKORC1

Important guideline group:
- CPIC = Clinical Pharmacogenetics Implementation Consortium

4. Phase I Enzyme Polymorphisms
-------------------------------
Phase I enzymes often modify drugs by oxidation, reduction, or hydrolysis.

Most important:
- Cytochrome P450 enzymes

Major pharmacogenomic CYP enzymes:
- CYP2D6
- CYP2C19
- CYP2C9
- CYP2B6

5. CYP2D6
---------
CYP2D6 metabolizes many clinically used drugs.

Important drug classes:
- Opioids
- Beta blockers
- Antidepressants
- Antipsychotics

Important alleles:
- Normal function: CYP2D6*1, *2
- Reduced function: CYP2D6*10, *17, *41
- No function: CYP2D6*3, *4, *5, *6
- Increased function: gene duplication or multiplication

Phenotypes:
- PM: little or no CYP2D6 activity
- IM: reduced activity
- EM: normal activity
- UM: increased activity

Clinical example: Codeine
-------------------------
Codeine is a prodrug.

Activation:
- Codeine → morphine via CYP2D6

Poor metabolizers:
- Little morphine formation
- Poor analgesic response

Ultrarapid metabolizers:
- Excess morphine formation
- Increased risk of sedation and respiratory depression

Recommendation:
- Avoid codeine in PMs and UMs.
- Use alternative analgesics such as morphine or nonopioid analgesics.

Other CYP2D6-related drugs:
- Tramadol
- Hydrocodone
- Oxycodone
- Metoprolol
- Nortriptyline
- Tamoxifen

Tamoxifen:
- Requires CYP2D6 activation to endoxifen.
- CYP2D6 poor metabolizers may have reduced therapeutic effect.

6. CYP2C19
----------
CYP2C19 metabolizes many acidic drugs.

Important drug classes:
- Proton pump inhibitors
- Antidepressants
- Antiepileptic drugs
- Antiplatelet drugs

Important alleles:
- Normal function: CYP2C19*1
- No function: CYP2C19*2, *3
- Increased function: CYP2C19*17

Clinical example: Clopidogrel
-----------------------------
Clopidogrel is a prodrug.

Activation:
- Requires CYP2C19 to form active thiol metabolite.

Poor or intermediate metabolizers:
- Reduced active metabolite formation
- Reduced antiplatelet effect
- Increased risk of stent thrombosis after PCI

Recommendation:
- Use alternative antiplatelet agents in PMs and IMs:
  - Prasugrel
  - Ticagrelor

Other CYP2C19 drugs:
- Omeprazole
- Diazepam
- Citalopram
- Voriconazole
- Mephenytoin

CYP2C19 poor metabolizers:
- Increased effect of omeprazole
- Increased toxicity risk with some substrates
- Reduced activation of clopidogrel

CYP2C19 ultrarapid metabolizers:
- Increased clopidogrel activation
- Increased bleeding risk
- Reduced efficacy of some drugs due to rapid clearance

7. CYP2B6
---------
CYP2B6 is highly polymorphic.

Important drug:
- Efavirenz

Important alleles:
- Normal: CYP2B6*1
- Decreased function: CYP2B6*6, *9
- No function: CYP2B6*18
- Increased function: CYP2B6*4

Clinical example: Efavirenz
---------------------------
Efavirenz is a nonnucleoside reverse transcriptase inhibitor used for HIV.

CYP2B6 metabolizes efavirenz.

Poor metabolizers:
- Higher efavirenz levels
- Increased CNS toxicity

CNS adverse effects:
- Sleep disturbance
- Impaired concentration
- Psychosis
- Depression
- Suicidal ideation

Recommendation:
- Consider reduced efavirenz dose in reduced-function CYP2B6 genotypes.

8. Dihydropyrimidine Dehydrogenase (DPD / DPYD)
-----------------------------------------------
DPD is encoded by DPYD.

Function:
- Catabolizes pyrimidines
- Major elimination pathway for fluoropyrimidines

Important drugs:
- 5-fluorouracil
- Capecitabine
- Tegafur

Important alleles:
- DPYD*2A
- DPYD*13
- rs67376798
- HapB3

Clinical significance:
- Reduced DPD activity causes reduced 5-FU clearance.
- Toxic metabolites accumulate.

Toxicities:
- Myelosuppression
- Mucositis
- Neurotoxicity
- Hand-foot syndrome
- Severe diarrhea

Recommendation:
- Reduced activity: reduce starting dose.
- Complete deficiency: avoid fluoropyrimidines.

9. Phase II Enzyme Polymorphisms
--------------------------------
Phase II enzymes conjugate drugs to enhance elimination.

Important enzymes:
- UGT1A1
- TPMT

10. UGT1A1
----------
UGT1A1 glucuronidates bilirubin and many drugs.

Important allele:
- UGT1A1*28

Effect:
- Reduced UGT1A1 expression
- Reduced bilirubin conjugation
- Increased unconjugated bilirubin

Clinical syndrome:
- Gilbert syndrome

Clinical example 1: Irinotecan
------------------------------
Irinotecan is a prodrug.

Activation:
- Irinotecan → SN-38

Inactivation:
- SN-38 is glucuronidated by UGT1A1.

UGT1A1*28 or *6:
- Reduced SN-38 clearance
- Increased risk of neutropenia and diarrhea

Recommendation:
- Reduce irinotecan starting dose in high-risk genotypes.

Clinical example 2: Atazanavir
------------------------------
Atazanavir inhibits UGT1A1.

Effect:
- Increased unconjugated bilirubin
- Jaundice

Patients with UGT1A1*28/*28:
- Higher risk of severe hyperbilirubinemia
- Higher risk of discontinuing atazanavir

Case answer:
- Yes, UGT1A1*28 polymorphism likely contributed to atazanavir-induced jaundice.

11. TPMT
--------
TPMT = thiopurine S-methyltransferase.

Function:
- Inactivates thiopurine drugs.

Important drugs:
- Azathioprine
- 6-mercaptopurine
- 6-thioguanine

Important alleles:
- TPMT*2
- TPMT*3A
- TPMT*3B
- TPMT*3C

Low TPMT activity:
- More active thioguanine nucleotides
- Increased bone marrow toxicity

Toxicity:
- Severe myelosuppression

Recommendations:
- Normal activity: standard dose
- Intermediate activity: reduce starting dose
- Low activity: major dose reduction or alternative therapy

12. NUDT15
----------
NUDT15 variants also affect thiopurine toxicity.

Important especially in:
- East Asian populations
- Some Hispanic populations

Effect:
- Increased thiopurine intolerance
- Increased myelosuppression risk

13. G6PD
--------
G6PD = glucose-6-phosphate dehydrogenase.

Function:
- Provides NADPH in red blood cells.
- Maintains reduced glutathione.
- Protects against oxidative stress.

G6PD deficiency:
- Reduced ability to handle oxidative stress.
- Risk of hemolysis.

Triggers:
- Infection
- Fava beans
- Oxidant drugs

Clinical example: Rasburicase
-----------------------------
Rasburicase converts uric acid to allantoin.

During this reaction:
- Hydrogen peroxide is produced.

G6PD deficiency:
- Increased risk of hemolytic anemia
- Increased risk of methemoglobinemia

Recommendation:
- Avoid rasburicase in G6PD deficiency.
- Use alternative such as allopurinol.

14. Genetic Variations in Transporters
--------------------------------------
Transporters influence drug absorption, distribution, and elimination.

Important transporter genes:
- SLCO1B1
- ABCG2
- SLC22A1

15. SLCO1B1 / OATP1B1
---------------------
OATP1B1 is a hepatic uptake transporter.

Encoded by:
- SLCO1B1

Substrates:
- Statins
- Methotrexate
- Bilirubin

Important variant:
- rs4149056
- Val174Ala

Effect:
- Reduced hepatic uptake
- Increased plasma statin levels
- Increased myopathy risk

Clinical example: Simvastatin
-----------------------------
Reduced-function SLCO1B1 variants:
- Increased simvastatin exposure
- Increased risk of simvastatin-induced myopathy

Recommendation:
- Use lower simvastatin dose or alternative statin.

Alternative statins:
- Pravastatin
- Rosuvastatin

16. ABCG2 / BCRP
----------------
BCRP is an efflux transporter.

Encoded by:
- ABCG2

Important variant:
- rs2231142
- Q141K

Effect:
- Reduced transporter function

Affected drugs:
- Rosuvastatin
- Allopurinol
- Some anticancer drugs

Clinical relevance:
- East Asian patients may have higher rosuvastatin levels.
- Lower rosuvastatin dose may be considered.

17. SLC22A1 / OCT1
------------------
OCT1 is a hepatic uptake transporter.

Substrates:
- Morphine
- Tramadol
- Sumatriptan
- Ondansetron

Reduced-function variants:
- May increase drug plasma concentrations.

18. Genetic Variation in Immune Function
----------------------------------------
Some adverse drug reactions are immune-mediated.

Important system:
- HLA genes

HLA variants can predict drug hypersensitivity.

19. HLA-B*57:01 and Abacavir
----------------------------
Drug:
- Abacavir

Risk:
- Severe hypersensitivity reaction
- Stevens-Johnson syndrome / toxic epidermal necrosis risk

Recommendation:
- Test for HLA-B*57:01 before starting abacavir.
- If positive, avoid abacavir.

20. HLA-B*15:02 and Carbamazepine
---------------------------------
Drug:
- Carbamazepine

Risk:
- Stevens-Johnson syndrome
- Toxic epidermal necrolysis

Important population:
- East Asian ancestry

Recommendation:
- Avoid carbamazepine in HLA-B*15:02-positive patients.

21. HLA-B*58:01 and Allopurinol
-------------------------------
Drug:
- Allopurinol

Risk:
- Severe cutaneous adverse reactions

Recommendation:
- Avoid allopurinol in HLA-B*58:01-positive patients.

22. HLA-B*57:01 and Flucloxacillin
----------------------------------
Drug:
- Flucloxacillin

Risk:
- Drug-induced liver injury

23. IFNL3 / IL28B
-----------------
IFNL3 variants affect response to hepatitis C treatment.

Important therapy:
- Pegylated interferon-alpha + ribavirin

Favorable genotype:
- Higher sustained virologic response

Unfavorable genotype:
- Lower cure rate

24. Polygenic Effects
---------------------
Many drug responses are influenced by more than one gene.

Example:
- Warfarin

Important genes:
- CYP2C9
- VKORC1

25. CYP2C9 and Warfarin
-----------------------
CYP2C9 metabolizes S-warfarin.

Important alleles:
- CYP2C9*2
- CYP2C9*3

Effect:
- Reduced warfarin metabolism
- Increased bleeding risk
- Lower dose requirement

26. VKORC1 and Warfarin
-----------------------
VKORC1 encodes vitamin K epoxide reductase.

Warfarin target:
- VKORC1

Important variant:
- VKORC1 -1639G>A

Effect:
- Reduced VKORC1 expression
- Increased warfarin sensitivity
- Lower dose requirement

Warfarin dosing:
- Best predicted using clinical factors + CYP2C9 + VKORC1.

27. Epigenomics
---------------
Epigenomics = heritable changes in gene expression not due to DNA sequence changes.

Mechanisms:
- DNA methylation
- Histone modification

Potential relevance:
- Drug response variability
- Acquired drug resistance
- Disease susceptibility

28. Case Study Summary
----------------------
Patient:
- HIV infection
- Treated with atazanavir + ritonavir + nucleoside analogs
- Developed jaundice after 1 year
- Grade 4 hyperbilirubinemia

Mechanism:
- Atazanavir inhibits UGT1A1.
- UGT1A1 normally glucuronidates bilirubin.
- Reduced UGT1A1 activity causes unconjugated bilirubin accumulation.

Genetic factor:
- UGT1A1*28/*28 reduces enzyme activity.

Conclusion:
- UGT1A1*28 polymorphism likely contributed to severe hyperbilirubinemia and jaundice.
- Switching from atazanavir to another antiretroviral improved bilirubin levels.

29. Must-Know Exam Pearls
-------------------------
- Pharmacogenomics guides precision medicine.
- CYP2D6 activates codeine to morphine.
- CYP2D6 poor metabolizers have poor codeine analgesia.
- CYP2D6 ultrarapid metabolizers risk morphine toxicity.
- CYP2C19 activates clopidogrel.
- CYP2C19 poor metabolizers have reduced clopidogrel response.
- CYP2B6 poor metabolizers have increased efavirenz toxicity.
- DPYD variants increase fluoropyrimidine toxicity.
- UGT1A1*28 increases irinotecan toxicity and atazanavir jaundice risk.
- TPMT deficiency increases thiopurine myelotoxicity.
- G6PD deficiency increases rasburicase hemolysis risk.
- SLCO1B1 variants increase simvastatin myopathy risk.
- HLA-B*57:01 predicts abacavir hypersensitivity.
- HLA-B*15:02 predicts carbamazepine SJS/TEN risk.
- HLA-B*58:01 predicts allopurinol severe skin reactions.
- Warfarin dosing is affected by CYP2C9 and VKORC1.
