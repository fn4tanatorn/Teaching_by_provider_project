Katzung Pharmacology Chapter 1 — Organized Notes
===================================================

1. Definitions
--------------
- Pharmacology: Study of substances interacting with living systems through chemical processes.
- Medical Pharmacology: Use of drugs to prevent, diagnose, and treat disease.
- Toxicology: Study of harmful effects of chemicals on living systems.

2. Major Divisions
------------------
A. Pharmacodynamics
   - What the drug does to the body
   - Drug-receptor interactions
   - Mechanism of action

B. Pharmacokinetics
   - What the body does to the drug
   - Absorption
   - Distribution
   - Metabolism
   - Excretion

3. Drug Properties
------------------
- Drugs may be solid, liquid, or gas.
- Most drugs are weak acids or weak bases.
- Molecular weight usually between 100–1000.

Important Factors:
- Size
- Shape
- Charge
- Lipid solubility
- Reactivity

4. Drug-Receptor Interactions
-----------------------------
Types of Bonds:
- Covalent (strong, often irreversible)
- Electrostatic
- Hydrophobic

Drug Types:
- Agonist → activates receptor
- Antagonist → blocks receptor
- Partial agonist → activates weakly
- Inverse agonist → produces opposite effect

5. Pharmacokinetic Principles
-----------------------------
Drug movement mechanisms (FIGURE 1-4):
- A — Aqueous diffusion through pores (paracellular)
- B — Lipid diffusion across membranes
- C — Carrier-mediated transport (incl. transporters; TABLE 1-3)
- D — Endocytosis / exocytosis

Fick’s Law:
- Drug movement depends on:
  * Concentration gradient
  * Surface area
  * Membrane thickness
  * Permeability

6. Weak Acids & Weak Bases
--------------------------
Henderson-Hasselbalch Equation:
- pH = pKa + log([A-]/[HA]) for weak acids; pH = pKa + log([BH+]/[B]) for weak bases.
- Predicts ionized vs unionized fraction — affects absorption and renal elimination.

Clinical Importance:
- Weak acids excreted faster in alkaline urine.
- Weak bases excreted faster in acidic urine.

Example:
- Aspirin overdose treated with sodium bicarbonate to alkalinize urine.

7. Drug Development Process
---------------------------
Stages:
1. Drug discovery
2. Preclinical testing
3. Clinical trials
4. FDA approval
5. Postmarketing surveillance

Clinical Trial Phases:
- Phase 1: Safety in healthy volunteers
- Phase 2: Efficacy in patients
- Phase 3: Large-scale testing
- Phase 4: Postmarketing monitoring

8. Preclinical Safety Testing
-----------------------------
- Acute toxicity
- Chronic toxicity
- Reproductive toxicity
- Carcinogenicity
- Mutagenicity

9. FDA Regulation
-----------------
Important Acts:
- Pure Food and Drug Act (1906)
- Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act (1938)
- Kefauver-Harris Amendments (1962)

Requirements:
- Drug must be safe and effective before marketing.

10. Adverse Drug Reactions
--------------------------
Examples:
- Allergy
- Toxicity
- Idiosyncratic reactions
- Drug interactions

11. Orphan Drugs
----------------
- Drugs developed for rare diseases.
- Supported by Orphan Drug Act (1983).

12. Key Concepts
----------------
- All substances can be toxic depending on dose.
- Drug selectivity depends on receptor binding.
- Pharmacology integrates physiology, biochemistry, genetics, and pathology.
