Adrenoceptor Agonists & Sympathomimetic Drugs
Chapter 9 High-Yield Output

1. High-Yield Summary
- Sympathomimetic drugs mimic sympathetic nervous system activity.
- They act by:
  - Direct receptor activation: epinephrine, norepinephrine, phenylephrine, dobutamine.
  - Indirect catecholamine enhancement: amphetamine, tyramine, cocaine.
  - Mixed action: ephedrine.
- Main receptors: alpha1, alpha2, beta1, beta2, beta3, D1, D2.
- Clinical themes:
  - alpha1 = vasoconstriction.
  - alpha2 = decreased sympathetic outflow.
  - beta1 = cardiac stimulation.
  - beta2 = bronchodilation and smooth muscle relaxation.
  - beta3 = bladder relaxation.
  - D1 = renal/splanchnic vasodilation.

2. Core Concepts

Adrenergic Receptors
Receptor | G protein | Main signaling | Key effects
alpha1 | Gq | ↑ IP3/DAG, ↑ Ca2+ | Vasoconstriction, mydriasis, urinary retention
alpha2 | Gi | ↓ cAMP | ↓ NE release, ↓ sympathetic outflow
beta1 | Gs | ↑ cAMP | ↑ HR, ↑ contractility, ↑ renin
beta2 | Gs | ↑ cAMP | Bronchodilation, vasodilation, uterine relaxation, ↑ K+ uptake
beta3 | Gs | ↑ cAMP | Bladder detrusor relaxation, lipolysis
D1 | Gs | ↑ cAMP | Renal/splanchnic vasodilation
D2 | Gi | ↓ cAMP | ↓ transmitter release

3. Key Mechanisms / Pathways

alpha1 pathway
- alpha1 → Gq → phospholipase C → IP3/DAG → ↑ intracellular Ca2+
- Result: smooth muscle contraction.
- Important sites: vascular smooth muscle, pupil dilator, bladder sphincter/prostate.

alpha2 pathway
- alpha2 → Gi → ↓ adenylyl cyclase → ↓ cAMP.
- Presynaptic alpha2 decreases norepinephrine release.
- CNS alpha2 agonists decrease sympathetic outflow.

beta pathway
- beta1/beta2/beta3 → Gs → ↑ adenylyl cyclase → ↑ cAMP.
- beta1 in heart: ↑ Ca2+ influx → ↑ heart rate and contractility.
- beta2 in smooth muscle: relaxation.
- beta3 in bladder: detrusor relaxation.

Dopamine pathway
- D1 → Gs → vasodilation, especially renal/splanchnic vessels.
- D2 → Gi → decreased neurotransmitter release.

4. Important Drugs / Molecules / Systems

Epinephrine
- Receptors: alpha1, alpha2, beta1, beta2.
- Effects:
  - ↑ systolic BP from beta1 cardiac stimulation.
  - ↓ diastolic BP at low doses due to beta2 vasodilation.
  - Bronchodilation via beta2.
  - Vasoconstriction via alpha1.
- Uses:
  - Anaphylaxis.
  - Cardiac arrest.
  - Added to local anesthetics to prolong action.
- Exam pearl: best drug for anaphylaxis because it treats airway, vascular collapse, and bronchospasm.

Norepinephrine
- Receptors: alpha1, alpha2, beta1.
- Minimal beta2 activity.
- Effects:
  - Strong vasoconstriction.
  - ↑ systolic and diastolic BP.
  - Reflex bradycardia may occur.
- Use: septic shock / severe hypotension.
- Exam pearl: strong pressor with reflex bradycardia.

Dopamine
- Dose-dependent effects:
  - Low dose: D1 → renal/splanchnic vasodilation.
  - Moderate dose: beta1 → ↑ contractility.
  - High dose: alpha1 → vasoconstriction.
- Use: shock historically; less favored than norepinephrine due to arrhythmia risk.

Phenylephrine
- Selective alpha1 agonist.
- Effects:
  - Vasoconstriction.
  - ↑ BP.
  - Reflex bradycardia.
  - Mydriasis without cycloplegia.
- Uses:
  - Nasal decongestant.
  - Mydriatic.
  - Hypotension.
- Toxicity: hypertension, reflex bradycardia.

Midodrine
- Oral prodrug → desglymidodrine.
- Selective alpha1 agonist.
- Use: orthostatic hypotension.
- Toxicity:
  - Supine hypertension.
  - Piloerection.
  - Urinary retention.
- Exam pearl: used in autonomic failure but can worsen urinary retention.

Clonidine
- alpha2 agonist.
- Mechanism: decreases CNS sympathetic outflow.
- Uses:
  - Hypertension.
  - ADHD.
  - Withdrawal syndromes.
- Toxicity:
  - Sedation.
  - Dry mouth.
  - Bradycardia.
  - Rebound hypertension if stopped suddenly.

Methyldopa
- alpha2 agonist.
- Use: hypertension in pregnancy.
- Toxicity: sedation, hemolytic anemia, hyperprolactinemia.

Dexmedetomidine
- alpha2 agonist.
- Use: ICU/procedural sedation.
- Benefit: sedation with less respiratory depression.

Tizanidine
- alpha2 agonist.
- Use: muscle spasticity.
- Pitfall: can cause hypotension and rebound hypertension.

Dobutamine
- Predominantly beta1 agonist.
- Effects:
  - ↑ contractility more than ↑ HR.
  - Increases cardiac output.
- Uses:
  - Acute heart failure.
  - Cardiogenic shock.
  - Pharmacologic cardiac stress testing.
- Toxicity: tachyarrhythmias, increased myocardial oxygen demand.

Isoproterenol
- Nonselective beta1 + beta2 agonist.
- Effects:
  - Marked tachycardia.
  - ↓ diastolic BP from beta2 vasodilation.
- Rarely used clinically.

Albuterol / Terbutaline
- beta2 agonists.
- Effects:
  - Bronchodilation.
  - Uterine relaxation.
  - Drives K+ into cells.
- Uses:
  - Asthma/COPD.
  - Hyperkalemia adjunct.
- Toxicity:
  - Tremor.
  - Tachycardia.
  - Hypokalemia.

Mirabegron
- beta3 agonist.
- Effect: relaxes detrusor muscle.
- Use: overactive bladder.
- Toxicity: hypertension.

Fenoldopam
- D1 agonist.
- Effect: renal and systemic vasodilation.
- Use: hypertensive emergency.
- Toxicity: hypotension, tachycardia, headache.

Amphetamine
- Indirect sympathomimetic.
- Mechanism: releases norepinephrine and dopamine.
- Uses:
  - ADHD.
  - Narcolepsy.
- Toxicity:
  - Insomnia.
  - Appetite suppression.
  - Hypertension.
  - Dependence/abuse.

Tyramine
- Indirect sympathomimetic found in aged cheese, cured meats, fermented foods.
- Normally metabolized by MAO.
- With MAO inhibitors → hypertensive crisis.
- Exam pearl: MAOI + tyramine = severe hypertension.

Cocaine
- Blocks reuptake of norepinephrine, dopamine, serotonin.
- Effects:
  - Sympathetic stimulation.
  - Vasoconstriction.
  - CNS stimulation.
- Toxicity:
  - Hypertension.
  - Coronary vasospasm.
  - MI.
  - Arrhythmias.
  - Seizures.

5. Clinical Correlations

Orthostatic hypotension in autonomic failure
- Failure of sympathetic compensation when standing.
- Findings:
  - Large BP drop on standing.
  - Inadequate HR increase.
  - Low plasma norepinephrine.
- Treatment:
  - Midodrine.
  - Droxidopa.
- Avoid:
  - alpha blockers such as tamsulosin.
  - unnecessary vasodilators.
- Important: patients are hypersensitive to pressor drugs.

Anaphylaxis
- Drug of choice: intramuscular epinephrine.
- Mechanisms:
  - alpha1: vasoconstriction, reduces edema, increases BP.
  - beta1: increases cardiac output.
  - beta2: bronchodilation, reduces mediator release.

Shock
- Preferred vasopressor in septic shock: norepinephrine.
- Dobutamine may be added when low cardiac output is present.

Nasal congestion
- alpha1 agonists constrict nasal mucosal vessels.
- Drugs: phenylephrine, oxymetazoline, pseudoephedrine.
- Pitfall: topical overuse → rebound congestion / rhinitis medicamentosa.

Asthma/COPD
- beta2 agonists cause bronchodilation.
- Short acting: albuterol.
- Long acting: salmeterol, formoterol.
- Ultra-long acting in COPD: indacaterol, olodaterol, vilanterol.

Horner syndrome pharmacology
- Postganglionic lesion: indirect agonists fail because catecholamine stores are lost.
- Direct agonists such as phenylephrine still dilate the pupil.

6. Adverse Effects / Toxicity / Pitfalls

Alpha1 agonists
- Hypertension.
- Reflex bradycardia.
- Urinary retention.
- Supine hypertension with midodrine.

Alpha2 agonists
- Sedation.
- Dry mouth.
- Bradycardia.
- Rebound hypertension if abruptly stopped.

Beta1 agonists
- Tachycardia.
- Arrhythmias.
- Increased myocardial oxygen demand.

Beta2 agonists
- Tremor.
- Tachycardia.
- Hypokalemia.
- Hyperglycemia.

Indirect sympathomimetics
- Hypertension.
- Insomnia.
- Anxiety.
- Abuse potential.
- Arrhythmias.

7. Important Comparisons

Epinephrine vs Norepinephrine
Feature | Epinephrine | Norepinephrine
Receptors | alpha1, alpha2, beta1, beta2 | alpha1, alpha2, beta1
beta2 activity | Strong | Minimal
Diastolic BP | May decrease at low dose | Increases
Heart rate | Increases | Reflex bradycardia possible
Best use | Anaphylaxis | Septic shock

Phenylephrine vs Isoproterenol
Feature | Phenylephrine | Isoproterenol
Main receptor | alpha1 | beta1, beta2
BP effect | ↑ BP | ↓ mean BP
HR effect | Reflex ↓ HR | Marked ↑ HR
Use | Decongestant, mydriasis, hypotension | Rare

Dobutamine vs Dopamine
Feature | Dobutamine | Dopamine
Main action | beta1 inotrope | Dose-dependent D1/beta1/alpha1
Use | Acute HF, stress test | Shock historically
Major risk | Tachyarrhythmia | More arrhythmias

Direct vs Indirect Sympathomimetics
Direct | Indirect
Bind receptors directly | Increase endogenous catecholamines
Work even if NE stores depleted | Require catecholamine stores
Examples: epinephrine, phenylephrine | Examples: amphetamine, tyramine, cocaine

8. Must-Know Exam Points
- alpha1 = vasoconstriction, mydriasis, urinary retention.
- alpha2 = decreased sympathetic outflow.
- beta1 = heart stimulation.
- beta2 = bronchodilation, uterine relaxation, hypokalemia.
- beta3 = bladder relaxation.
- D1 = renal vasodilation.
- Epinephrine is first-line for anaphylaxis.
- Norepinephrine is preferred vasopressor in septic shock.
- Phenylephrine causes reflex bradycardia.
- Midodrine treats orthostatic hypotension but can cause supine hypertension.
- Clonidine withdrawal causes rebound hypertension.
- MAOI + tyramine causes hypertensive crisis.
- Cocaine blocks catecholamine reuptake and can cause MI.
- Dobutamine is used in cardiac stress testing and acute heart failure.
- Albuterol toxicity includes tremor, tachycardia, and hypokalemia.
- Mirabegron treats overactive bladder via beta3 activation.

9. Case-Based Integration

Diagnosis
- Pure autonomic failure causing neurogenic orthostatic hypotension.

Key clues
- Lightheadedness on standing.
- Worse after meals/hot environments.
- Supine hypertension with standing hypotension.
- Inadequate compensatory HR rise.
- Low norepinephrine.
- Autonomic symptoms: constipation, urinary retention, decreased sweating.
- No diabetes, Parkinson disease, or drug cause.

Mechanism
- Loss of peripheral autonomic sympathetic fibers.
- Inadequate norepinephrine release on standing.
- Failure of reflex vasoconstriction.
- Blood pools in lower body → decreased venous return → orthostatic hypotension.

Why tamsulosin worsened symptoms
- Tamsulosin blocks alpha1A receptors.
- Reduces sympathetic vascular/urinary tract tone.
- In autonomic failure, BP depends on limited residual sympathetic tone.
- Blocking alpha receptors worsens orthostatic hypotension.

Treatment concept
- Use pressor agents carefully.
- Midodrine can improve standing BP.
- Droxidopa can increase norepinephrine availability.
- Monitor for supine hypertension.
- Avoid OTC sympathomimetics unless supervised.

One-line board review:
Sympathomimetics increase sympathetic signaling: alpha1 squeezes vessels, alpha2 shuts down sympathetic outflow, beta1 boosts the heart, beta2 opens lungs, beta3 relaxes bladder, and D1 dilates renal vessels.
